The experiment has multiple rounds. In the experiment, participants will be equally divided into two roles: player A and player B. Players interact with each other in a virtual market, where player B sells their products to player A. Your role does not change during the experiment. Participants are divided into groups of 4 people with 2 player A and 2 player B. You will be grouped with the same participants during the experiment.

Each round has multiple stages as follows:
Stage 1:
All player B choose whether to enter the market.
If player B chooses to stay outside the market, her round ends.
If player B chooses to enter the market, she will move to Stage 2.
Stage 2:
If the experiment allows, player B who entered the market at Stage 1 can choose the price of her product.
Otherwise price will be given by the system.
Stage 3:
Observing player B's choices of products in the past rounds and price in the current round, Player A chooses which player B to purchase from.
Player A can only purchase from player B who enter the market in the current round.
Player A can also choose to buy nothing and earn a reserved payoff.
Stage 4:
If player B receives purchase one or more player A, she chooses to produce X or Y, which determined the payoff both player.

The results will be calculated after stage 4. All participants receive the payoff in the current round and the experiment moves to the next round. In the next round, the experiment will repeat Stage 1 - 4 but the history of player B will remain observable to player A.
